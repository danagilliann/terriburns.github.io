I'm Terri, and I love Slack. A bit about me: I'm a senior studying computer science at NYU. I work part time as a developer at Venmo, and am President of Tech@NYU, NYC's largest student technology organization. I lead a team of 25 incredible students to ship products and host events to strengthen the NYC tech community. I'm interested in product management, building diverse tech communities, and making life simpler and more efficient.
Slack has changed the way I communicate with Internet friends, IRL friends, coworkers, and fellow students. I am proud to see the tangible impact Slack has on improving people's day to day interactions. Here are some of my thoughts on directions Slack can go in the future to be even greater.

S T U D E N T S

🌀 🍬 🍋 🍎 🍊 🌙 🌰

About half of the slack groups that I am in consist almost entirely of students.
Very recently a friend of mine from school (NYU) told me that they were using Slack for class discussions. I was really excited to hear this because it was a friend in a non-technical major, and most of the Slack groups that I am a part of are for technical communitities.
Slack has the potential to really improve communication for educational institutions, which is currently a desperate need. In educational settings, turnaround time for events, projects, meetings, classes, etc. are much faster than in corporate/professional settings. Similar to Google Apps for education, schools could utlize a "Slack for Education" account. This would essentially be a master Slack group with highly-structured admin setings. The channels in the group could be used for classes, school events, important announcements, etc. Achieving this would be a huge technical feat; it's a complex system to implement and think through, but has the potential to impact the education system in a really powerful way.

C H A N N E L S

🎅 🎆 🎇 🎈 🎉 🎊 🎋

Slack gets cluttered, fast.
The Slack group that I am most active in is for Tech@NYU NYC's largest student technology organization, which I lead. We have periodically gone on Slack "cleanses," where we wipe out channels that go unused. A small, but potentially really helpful suggestion, would be to have Slackbot automatically message a channel asking if it should be archived, conditional upon innactivity for a certain period of time (say, 2 weeks. This feature could also come with more settings to regulate the parameters for how this works.) Having fewer, more meaningful channels can help avoid users feeling overwhelemed when using Slack.
Additionally, a really important feature for Slack would be one that would allow Slack to jump straight to your mention in a channel. I regularly get mentioned in really busy channels, and find myself having to dig through a ton of messages to (only sometimes successfully) find the original mention.

S L A C K B O T S

🍖 🍗 🍘 🍈 🍓 🍔 🍕

Slackbots have enhanced my slack experience in almost every channel that I'm in.
Slack should encourage users (specifically non-technical users) to make bots! Bots are so useful because they can be impleneted to do pretty much anything necessary for a specific group. I recently read this great medium article that is a step closer towards that.
